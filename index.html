Trading Aces

An app that offers the opportunity to help people within your community by providing a digital tool to share services without spending money.

Role

UX Designer: User Research, Definition and Synthesis, Ideation, Wireframing, Prototyping, and Iterations.

Timeline

October 2021

Tools

UX Designer: User Research, Definition and Synthesis, Ideation, Wireframing, Prototyping, and Iterations.

Overview

During my UX UI Bootcamp in a team of three we were asked to create a brand-new mobile application. The mobile application needed to include solving a real, clearly identified need in users’ everyday lives. We created an app that offers the opportunity to help people within your community by providing a digital tool to share services without spending money.

Problem Statement

Not everyone has the financial resources to pay for services when they need it. How might there be an alternative way to get help without spending money?

User Research

I shared a survey and as a team we interviewed 4 individuals over the age of 18 which led us to finding: 100% of respondents from the survey were interested in the app. Some main points users mentioned during user interviews were: “I could post on Facebook marketplace but that isn’t a trade!”- Abby O. “This would be really helpful for people in need or people who don’t have the resources”- Hazheen J. How could we find a fair way to mediate the trades digitally? "Creating a reward system for people who hold up their end"- Tyler B.

Proto Persona

Based off our survey and user interviews as a team we created a proto persona to adequately represent our target users needs and traits.

Competitor Analysis

I completed a competitor analysis. I found it difficult to find current apps close to our idea. Fiverr was the closest to our idea but you have to pay for services instead of trading for them.

Empathy Map

As a team we created an empathy map based on our findings to accurately empathize with users. Throughout the design process I consistently tried to empathize with Micah and her needs.

Value Proposition

Trading Aces helps users feel like their skills are being used to help one another while they are saving money.

Story Time

To better understand users we developed a storyboard to tell Micah's story. Telling a story helped us to better understand Micah and her needs.

Paper Prototyping

As a team we all created our own paper sketches and then took parts from all of them and combine them into one design. After we came up with a design we used the marvel app to do user testing on our sketches to find any small issues before we started wireframing.
